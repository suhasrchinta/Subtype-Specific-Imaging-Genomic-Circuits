CIRCUIT 01 OF 03

HR+/HER2-: the estrogen-suppression circuit

Patient 331192. 24 pCR cases in this subtype across the cohort; treat this circuit's findings as preliminary.

01

The scan, timepoint by timepoint

This is a real signal-enhancement map (SER) from patient 331192's DCE-MRI, with the tumor segmentation outlined in red. Four scans were taken across neoadjuvant treatment: baseline (T0), then three follow-ups (T1-T3).

02

The tumor signal collapses

The enhancing tumor region shrinks from 1,049 signal-positive pixels at baseline to just 55 by T3, a decline mirrored in this patient's tabular functional tumor volume (FTV): 58.6 to 0.21. This is what a complete responder's scan looks like.

03

That decline correlates with a gene

Across 161 HR+/HER2- patients, early FTV change correlates with expression of SCUBE2, a gene with an established role in hormone-responsive luminal epithelial biology. Each dot below is one real patient.

04

SCUBE2 sits inside a suppressed pathway

Gene set enrichment analysis places SCUBE2 within the Estrogen Response pathway, which is suppressed in patients who respond (NES = -2.821). Imaging feature, gene, and pathway now form a complete circuit.

05

Is this statistically real, or a coincidence?

Formal bootstrapped mediation testing was used to check whether SCUBE2 statistically explains part of the imaging-outcome relationship, not just correlates with it. The result is directionally consistent but weaker than the circuit below.

06

Does it hold up outside this dataset?

SCUBE2's gene-outcome relationship was tested for replication in three independent external cohorts, on entirely different patients than the discovery data above.

07

How does a scan become a number?

Before any of this, the raw scan is reduced to a single quantitative feature: this patient's early functional tumor volume (FTV) change, extracted directly from the segmented signal-enhancement map.

08

How does the model turn that into a prediction?

That feature, alongside 27 others describing tumor volume, shape, and enhancement over time, feeds a Random Forest of 500 decision trees. Each tree votes; the majority vote produces this subtype's AUC of 0.699.

CIRCUIT 02 OF 03

HER2+: the Hedgehog-suppression circuit

Patient 474874. The strongest single mediation result in the study.

01

A different subtype, a different pattern

Patient 474874's real SER scan across four timepoints. In HER2+ disease, the imaging feature that matters most is the tumor's longest diameter (LD) and its early volume change, not FTV alone.

02

A gradual, real regression

Signal-positive pixels decline from 4,712 at baseline to 227 by T3, tracking this patient's tabular longest-diameter decline from 7.6mm to fully resolved.

03

The strongest gene relationship in this study

Across 90 HER2+ patients, early FTV change correlates with ERBB2 and its co-amplified neighbor GRB7. Both sit at the same 17q12 locus that defines HER2-positive disease.

04

Suppressing Hedgehog, activating interferon

ERBB2/GRB7 sit within a suppressed Hedgehog signaling pathway (NES = -2.031), alongside activation of interferon-alpha and interferon-gamma response signatures, a pattern distinct from the estrogen-driven HR+/HER2- circuit.

05

The strongest mediation result in the study

ERBB2 is the only circuit gene significant under both two-sided and pre-specified one-sided mediation tests: an estimated 23.1% of the imaging-outcome association is statistically explained by ERBB2 expression.

06

A more complicated replication story

ERBB2 was not individually significant in any single external cohort, but was directionally consistent in all three. Combined-evidence testing recovers the signal, a case for looking across cohorts, not just within one.

07

How does a scan become a number?

This patient's longest-diameter decline (7.6mm to fully resolved) is extracted directly from the segmented scan as a single quantitative feature before any modeling happens.

08

How does the model turn that into a prediction?

That feature joins the same 28-feature panel used across all subtypes, feeding a Random Forest of 500 trees. For HER2+ disease, the majority vote produces an AUC of 0.754.

CIRCUIT 03 OF 03

Triple-negative: the vascular / interferon circuit

Patient 221686. A structurally different kind of evidence than the first two circuits.

01

A vascular signal, not a tumor signal

Patient 221686's real SER scan. In TNBC, the dominant imaging feature is background parenchymal enhancement (BPE), signal in the normal tissue around the tumor, reflecting vascular and hormonal activity rather than the tumor itself.

02

A strong, real decline

Signal-positive pixels fall from 2,793 at baseline to 160 by T3, tracking this patient's BPE decline of roughly 67 percent over treatment.

03

An honest, weak scatter, and that is the point

Across 131 TNBC patients, BPE shows only a near-zero direct correlation with FLT1. That is not a bug in this demo. The source paper explicitly reports that BPE carries zero genome-wide significant gene associations at the strict Level 2 test, direct evidence that this imaging feature is vascular, not tumor-transcriptomic.

04

The circuit exists at the pathway level instead

FLT1 and ANGPT2 sit within an activated interferon-alpha pathway (NES = +4.265), established through targeted gene-set testing, not a raw scatter relationship. FLT1 and ANGPT2 are vascular endothelial genes, not tumor-intrinsic like SCUBE2 or ERBB2.

05

Underpowered, not disproven

FLT1's mediation test used only 27 patients, the IVIM imaging subset, giving roughly 10 percent statistical power. The result is reported honestly as underpowered rather than as a null finding.

06

A real signal and a clean null, side by side

FLT1 replicated in 1 of 3 external cohorts. ANGPT2, its shared-pathway neighbor, showed a clean null across all three, sharpening rather than muddying the picture.

07

How does a scan become a number?

This patient's background parenchymal enhancement decline (about 67 percent over treatment) is extracted directly from the scan as a single quantitative feature.

08

How does the model turn that into a prediction?

That feature joins the same 28-feature panel, feeding a Random Forest of 500 trees. For TNBC, the majority vote produces the strongest subtype AUC in the study: 0.787.

APPENDIX · HOW TO READ THIS

What this page shows, and doesn't

Real data, real patients

Every scan, gene-correlation point, and AUC value on this page comes directly from the I-SPY2/ACRIN-6698 cohort (n=384) and its linked GSE194040 gene expression data (n=988). Nothing here is simulated.

Mediation, not causation

The paper's circuits are established via formal bootstrapped mediation analysis, not simple correlation. Some relationships shown here are directionally consistent but statistically weaker than the strongest circuit (ERBB2). That nuance is preserved rather than smoothed over.

TNBC's circuit is different in kind

FLT1 and ANGPT2 are vascular/microenvironment genes, not tumor-intrinsic like SCUBE2 or ERBB2. Background parenchymal enhancement shows no significant genome-wide gene correlation on its own. The TNBC circuit is established at the pathway level.

Preliminary findings

HR+/HER2- results are based on only 24 pCR-positive cases and are reported as preliminary throughout the source study. This page carries that same caveat forward.
